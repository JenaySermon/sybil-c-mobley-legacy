About Dr. Mobley
October 14, 1925 — September 29, 2015

Dr. Sybil Collins Mobley

Dr. Sybil Collins Mobley began her career at Florida A&M University as a clerk-typist in 1945 and retired fifty-eight years later as Dean Emerita of a business school she built from the ground up.

In 1974, she founded the School of Business and Industry (SBI) — a program that combined rigorous academics, mandatory corporate internships, and a world-renowned Professional Leadership Development program to produce graduates working in 60 countries.

She served on the boards of seven Fortune 500 companies, consulted for the U.S. government across Africa, and personally recruited generations of students she called her "SBI Superstars." She did not take no from anyone who didn't have the authority to say yes.

Our Mission

Why we exist.

To preserve, protect, and authentically advance the legacy, vision, and impact of Dr. Sybil C. Mobley — ensuring that her contributions to business education, racial equity, and Black excellence continue to inspire and shape future generations.

The Legacy Project operates with one guiding conviction, from the words Dr. Mobley made famous among generations of students: "No excuse is acceptable." We steward her name, her story, and her standards with the same rigor and integrity she brought to every institution she touched.

Our Vision

What we're building.

A world where Dr. Mobley's pioneering model of excellence — fusing academic mastery, corporate access, and unapologetic Black ambition — is recognized, protected, and replicated as a blueprint for equitable economic transformation.

We envision a nationally recognized institution that carries her innovations forward: into entrepreneurship, into technology, into community development, and into the next generation of leaders she never stopped believing in.

"No excuse is acceptable. No amount of effort is adequate until proven effective." — Dr. Sybil C. Mobley Founding Principle
Programs & Focus Areas

Five interconnected lines of work.

All rooted in the values, innovations, and unfinished work of Dr. Mobley herself.

Program 01

Legacy Stewardship & Documentation

Preserving Dr. Mobley's archives, oral histories, academic contributions, and institutional records. We ensure authenticity in how her name, likeness, and contributions are represented across institutions, partners, media, and the public record.

Program 02

Scholar Pipeline & Youth Business Education

Supporting emerging business talent through scholarship initiatives, the Sybil C. Mobley Summer Business Institute model, and partnerships that connect students directly to corporate opportunity — honoring her original vision of access plus excellence.

Program 03

Entrepreneurship & Innovation

Advancing economic empowerment in Black and Brown communities through small business development, technology in business and accounting, and innovations in alternative economic systems — a direct extension of her lifelong entrepreneurial vision.

Program 04

Institutional Accountability & Partnership

Engaging universities, corporations, and civic institutions that carry her name with clarity, respect, and shared standards — ensuring that endowed chairs, named programs, and legacy initiatives authentically reflect the founding principles she established.

Program 05 · Family Council

Family Legacy Council Initiatives

Coordinating unified family representation in formal engagements, and supporting legacy-aligned real estate, cultural, and community projects that extend her vision into the built environment and the next generation.
